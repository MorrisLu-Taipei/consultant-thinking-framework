# Consulting Thinking Capability System
*An operationalized framework for consulting thinking development and assessment*

Author: Morris (Yeh-Hsing) Lu · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/morris-lu-89144821/) · ORCID 0009-0006-5373-0586 · Text: CC BY-NC-ND 4.0

> **North star.** I don't want you to remember what the client said — I want you to judge what the client truly needs, and how we win the next step.

**Honest positioning.** This is not a new strategy framework. It is a system that makes consulting thinking *trainable and scorable, with effects verifiable through pre/post testing*. It builds on Ohmae's 3C, TOWS, Temporal Competitive Analysis, and the Kano model; its contribution is (a) placing subject × time on one orthogonal grid, and (b) selective "diagonal" synthesis. It has **not yet been empirically validated**.

## 1. The matrix
Four lenses — **Customer, Competitor, Company (us), Environment** — across three times: **Past (cause), Present (need), Future (solution)**. Mark each cell:
- 📥 **Customer input** = raw material you hear (given to you).
- 🧠 **Consultant output** = what you produce (where the value is).

Only the customer's past/present *surface* is 📥 input; the other cells are mostly 🧠 output. A C1 person can only fill the 📥 cells (copying the client); the value is entirely in the 🧠 cells **+ the diagonal**.

## 2. Three readings
- **Vertical = vector**: derive the future from "what force pushed the past into the present" — don't guess.
- **Horizontal = gap**: asymmetry across customer/competitor/us/environment is the opportunity.
- **Diagonal = the fee**: connect across subject × across time, e.g. *competitor's present blind spot × customer's future need × environment's past shift → recommendation*.
> Filling boxes is not thinking; the value is in the diagonal.

## 3. Capability ladder
C1 Record → C2 Organize → C3 Analyze → C4 Plan (one diagonal) → C5 Advise (multiple diagonals + unspoken need + us/environment). Most people are stuck at C1.

## 4. The consulting summary (six parts)
1. Customer's exact words (facts). 2. True pain (surface → root). 3. Present need (the job to be done). 4. Future recommendation **+ the unspoken need**. 5. Competition & environment. 6. How we enter (can / cannot solve; which standard product). Plus: **next step** and **at least one explicit diagonal**.

## 5. Pre-submit rule
If your report contains no sentence like "**because the competitor now…, plus the environment trend…, the customer will in future…**", it is not ready. In scoring: **no diagonal caps the level at C3; only answering what was asked caps it at C4.**

## 6. Using it with AI
You don't need to memorize prompts — just say "critique these meeting notes with the consulting thinking matrix" or "score this with the rubric." Discipline: separate **signal vs inference**, never fabricate, mark anything unverified as **TBD**.
